Butter Chicken

Butter chicken, or murgh makhani, is a classic Indian dish made with tender chicken pieces cooked in a creamy, mildly spiced tomato sauce. The origins of butter chicken can be traced back to Delhi, India, where it was created by the chefs of the Moti Mahal restaurant in the 1950s. The dish was reportedly invented by chance when leftover tandoori chicken was cooked in a rich, buttery tomato gravy. Butter chicken is known for its rich, velvety texture and mild, buttery flavor. It is typically served with naan or rice and garnished with fresh cilantro. Butter chicken has become a popular dish worldwide and is a favorite in Indian restaurants around the globe.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds boneless chicken thighs, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons garam masala
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons ginger paste
  • 2 cups tomato puree
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Steps

  1. Marinate chicken in yogurt and garam masala for 2 hours
  2. Cook marinated chicken in butter until golden brown
  3. Saut√© onions, garlic, and ginger until fragrant
  4. Add tomato puree and simmer for 15 minutes
  5. Stir in heavy cream and cooked chicken
  6. Simmer for additional 10 minutes
  7. Garnish with cilantro and serve with naan bread
